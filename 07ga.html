07GA
Bozeman, Montana




Residential
2020 - 2022
Completed




The client desired a structure that could function as either a vacation retreat, or a single-family home. The project was to replace an existing structure on a small, corner parcel near the mountain town of Bozeman, Montana.

Due to Montana’s erratic seasonal climate which experiences an arid summer, and a harsh winter the home needed to accommodate a diptych lifestyle. Summers are warm and lazy - a pleasant time to leave your doors and windows open for the afternoon breeze, or an evening meal on your patio. Winter is different however. With temperatures that often drop below -30 °F the house needed to also be a cozy den as well.

Resisting the urge to develop a novel form, a decision was made to build within what was the original building footprint to minimize the new construction’s impact on neighborhood character.

This created interesting design opportunities and obstacles, one of which was the challenge of fitting a two car garage on a site that could not support a traditional two car garage given the original building footprint’s interface with certain unalterable site conditions. The garage thus becomes a two-car sally port that can easily house a truck with a raft or driftboat hitched behind, or a space that can be segmented so that two parties might have access to a private garage. Additionally, the garage encloses the backyard creating a secluded garden area.
